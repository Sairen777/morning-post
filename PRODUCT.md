# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Morning Post is for individual readers who follow several Telegram channels and
Substack publications and want to review them without scanning every source.
They usually arrive with limited attention, need to understand what matters, and
may decide which full article to read or which publication deserves a paid
subscription.

## Product Purpose

Morning Post collects subscribed content, preserves source context, and turns it
into a concise, trustworthy digest. It should reduce reading effort without
hiding provenance or overstating content the user cannot access. Success means a
reader can scan the digest, understand the important material, and choose their
next reading or subscription action with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Calm, editorial, trustworthy. The product should feel considered and
reader-first, with concise language and restrained visual emphasis.

## Anti-references

- Engagement-driven social feeds with noisy activity, urgency, or
  infinite-scroll cues.
- Subscription upsells that pressure the reader or imply an endorsement.
- Generic SaaS dashboards built from repetitive or nested cards.
- Decorative controls or motion that compete with the reading task.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve provenance: titles, publications, timestamps, and links should
   remain easy to verify.
2. Respect access boundaries: distinguish full content, previews, and
   inaccessible paid posts without pretending they are equivalent.
3. Inform without pressuring: subscription discovery should present evidence and
   let the reader decide.
4. Keep the reading hierarchy quiet: source, article, and supporting metadata
   should be clear without visual clutter.
5. Prefer familiar controls: settings and actions should behave like standard
   product UI and explain their consequences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. All workflows must support keyboard navigation, visible
focus, sufficient text and control contrast, semantic status and error
announcements, and reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone to
communicate access or loading state.
